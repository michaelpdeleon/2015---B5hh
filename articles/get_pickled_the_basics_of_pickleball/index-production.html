Get Pickled: The Basics of Pickleball

By: [Author]

Last Updated: Mar. 23, 2015

 

In This Article: What is Pickleball? How is Pickleball Played?

Pickleball has been gaining popularity in recent years with more and more people getting pickled or becoming fans of the sport. A game that combines aspects of badminton, tennis and table tennis, pickleball can be played by just about anyone. This fast moving and fun game is a great low-impact workout, making it increasing popular with the 50 and older crowd. As there are not a lot of rules to remember, you too can be pickled in no time.

 What is Pickleball?

Simply put, pickleball is a racket sport that was invented in the mid-1960’s by a Washington State Congressman and his family. As the story goes, the family had a badminton court but no badminton rackets so they improvised with table tennis paddles and a perforated ball. Over the years, this children’s backyard game has become popular among adults as a fun game for a variety of athletic levels. There are multiple theories about how the game got the name Pickleball. The most popular is the story of the family dog, Pickles, chasing after the ball as the family played. Another explanation is that the game is named after the Pickle Boat in crew. A Pickle Boat is made up of oarsmen who are not needed for the other boats, similar to the game of pickleball that combines elements of multiple racket sports.

 How is Pickleball Played?

Both an indoor and outdoor game, pickleball is played on a badminton-sized court with a net and rules similar to tennis. The pickleball is served underhand and must bounce once before the opposing team hits the ball and then once again after it is returned. Points can only be scored by the serving team with most games going up to eleven points. Like tennis, each game must be won by a margin of two points. This fast-paced game can be played by doubles or singles.

Easy to learn, pickleball does not require much gear. The following essentials will have you playing in no time:

  • A pickleball paddle made of wood or composite materials, to hit a perforated ball, similar to a wiffle ball, over a net.
  • The net should be 34” high in the center, 2” lower than a tennis net, and strung across a badminton sized court.
  • Unlike tennis, there are no designated pickleball clothes. Comfortable shorts, pants and tops that allow for movement are ideal for pickleball. Athletic, tennis-style or court shoes should be worn while playing.
